Daily Life

Daily Life

8 minutes read
Intermediate Level
For New Muslims

Islamic lifestyle is not limited to worship alone. Islam teaches living with good character, justice, mercy, and honesty in every aspect of life.

Beginning of the Day

A Muslim should adopt these habits at the beginning of each day:

Waking Up

  • Supplication: "Alhamdulillahil-ladhi ahyana ba'da ma amatana wa ilayhin-nushur"
  • Thank Allah
  • Start the day positively

Morning Prayer

  • Performed at Fajr time
  • For a blessed start to the day
  • Connecting with Allah

Reading Quran

  • At least one page daily
  • Try to learn the meaning
  • Reflect and contemplate

Food Etiquette

In Islam, eating is also an act of worship with specific etiquettes:

Before Eating

  • Say "Bismillah"
  • Wash your hands
  • Sit in a clean place
  • Eat with the right hand

While Eating

  • Take small bites
  • Chew slowly
  • Avoid waste
  • Love to share

After Eating

  • Say "Alhamdulillahil-ladhi at'amana wa saqana"
  • Thank Allah
  • Clean up leftovers
  • Wash your hands

Social Relationships

Islam encourages good character in interpersonal relationships:

Family Relationships

  • Respect for parents: Paradise lies at the feet of mothers
  • Kind treatment of spouse: Mutual love and respect
  • Mercy to children: Raise them with good character
  • Family ties: Maintain kinship bonds

Neighborhood Relations

  • Kindness to neighbors: "He is not one of us whose neighbor goes to bed hungry"
  • Avoid harm: No noise, smell, or other disturbance
  • Mutual help: Support in times of need
  • Greeting: Meet with a smiling face

Community Relations

  • Justice: Give everyone their rights
  • Honesty: Keep your word, protect trusts
  • Mercy: Help the weak
  • Tolerance: Respect differences

Work and Business Ethics

Islam sets high moral standards in business life as well:

Honesty

Give accurate information in transactions, don't hide product defects

Justice

No cheating in weights and measures, set fair prices

Trust

Protect customer's money and goods

Avoiding Haram Income

No interest, gambling, or trading in forbidden goods

Personal Hygiene and Health

Islam considers cleanliness as half of faith:

Body Cleanliness

  • Regular bathing (ghusl)
  • Dental hygiene (using miswak)
  • Trimming nails
  • Wearing clean clothes

Healthy Living

  • Balanced nutrition
  • Avoid excess
  • Regular exercise
  • Early to bed, early to rise

Dhikr and Supplication

Remembering Allah and making supplication in daily life:

Morning Dhikr

  • Subhanallah (33 times)
  • Alhamdulillah (33 times)
  • Allahu Akbar (34 times)
  • Recite Ayat al-Kursi

Evening Dhikr

  • Seek forgiveness (istighfar)
  • Send blessings on the Prophet
  • Express gratitude for the day
  • Repent (tawbah)

Coping with Difficult Times

Islam teaches patience and trust in Allah during difficult moments:

Patience (Sabr)

Trust in Allah's wisdom and avoid hasty actions

Supplication (Dua)

Seek help from Allah and take refuge in Him

Trust (Tawakkul)

After doing your best, leave the result to Allah

Gratitude (Shukr)

Be grateful for what you have and look positively

End of the Day

Things to do at the end of the day:

Maghrib Prayer

The obligatory prayer performed at sunset

Isha Prayer

The last obligatory prayer of the day

Self-Reflection

Evaluate the day, learn from mistakes

Sleep Supplication

Say "Bismika Allahumma amutu wa ahya"

Practical Advice

For New Muslims

  • Learn step by step, don't rush
  • Connect with local Muslim community
  • Don't hesitate to ask questions
  • Be patient and don't force yourself

Daily Routines

  • Save prayer times on your phone
  • Develop Quran reading habit
  • Read Islamic books
  • Build righteous friendships

Conclusion

Islamic lifestyle means remembering Allah in every moment of life and striving to earn His pleasure. This lifestyle brings peace both in this world and the hereafter.

Remember that striving for perfection is beautiful, but don't blame yourself when you make mistakes. Allah is Most Forgiving and Most Merciful.
