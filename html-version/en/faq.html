FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

12 minutes read
All Levels
For New Muslims

The most commonly asked questions by new Muslims with detailed answers. Find answers to your questions about Islam.

Basic Faith Questions

What do I need to do to become Muslim?

To become Muslim, you need to recite the Shahada (Declaration of Faith): "Ash-hadu an la ilaha illa Allah, wa ash-hadu anna Muhammadan rasul Allah" (I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His messenger).

When you sincerely say these words, you become Muslim. After that, you can start learning the fundamentals of Islam.

What are the Five Pillars of Islam?

The five fundamental pillars of Islam are:

  • Shahada: Believing in Allah's oneness and Muhammad's prophethood
  • Salah: Performing five daily prayers
  • Zakat: Giving financial assistance to those who meet certain conditions
  • Sawm: Fasting during the month of Ramadan
  • Hajj: Pilgrimage to Mecca for those who are able

What are the Six Pillars of Faith?

Islam has six pillars of faith:

  • Faith in Allah
  • Faith in Angels
  • Faith in Books
  • Faith in Prophets
  • Faith in the Day of Judgment
  • Faith in Destiny

Prayer and Worship Questions

How can I learn to pray?

To learn prayer, you can follow this step-by-step process:

  • First learn how to perform ablution
  • Learn the prayer times
  • Determine the Qibla direction
  • Learn prayer positions (standing, bowing, prostrating)
  • Learn Surah Al-Fatiha and basic supplications
  • Get help from your local mosque

How can I learn prayer times?

Prayer times are determined by the sun's position:

  • Fajr (Dawn): Before dawn breaks
  • Dhuhr (Noon): After the sun passes its zenith
  • Asr (Afternoon): In the afternoon
  • Maghrib (Sunset): After sunset
  • Isha (Night): When evening twilight disappears

You can download a prayer time app on your phone to track daily times.

How do I perform ablution?

Steps for performing ablution:

  1. Say "Bismillah"
  2. Wash your hands three times
  3. Rinse your mouth three times
  4. Clean your nose three times
  5. Wash your face three times
  6. Wash your arms up to the elbows three times
  7. Wipe your head
  8. Wash your feet up to the ankles three times

Daily Life Questions

Which foods are halal and which are haram?

Halal foods:

  • Vegetables and fruits
  • Grains and legumes
  • Halal slaughtered meat (beef, lamb, chicken, etc.)
  • Fish and seafood
  • Milk and dairy products

Haram foods:

  • Pork and pork products
  • Alcoholic beverages
  • Non-halal slaughtered animal meat
  • Dead animal meat

How can I tell my family that I became Muslim?

This is a sensitive matter and may require different approaches for each situation:

  • Be patient and don't rush
  • First learn Islam well
  • Set an example with your good behavior
  • Wait for the right time
  • Use respectful and understanding language
  • If necessary, seek advice from experienced Muslims

What are women's rights in Islam?

Islam grants many rights to women:

  • Right to education
  • Right to work
  • Right to inheritance
  • Marriage and divorce rights
  • Property rights
  • Right to political participation

Islam views men and women in different roles but of equal value.

Social and Cultural Questions

How can I go to the mosque?

For your first visit to a mosque:

  • Wear clean clothes
  • Remove your shoes at the entrance
  • Enter quietly
  • Explain your situation to the imam or congregation
  • Don't hesitate to ask for help
  • If you don't know how to pray, you can just observe

How can I make Muslim friends?

To connect with the Muslim community:

  • Regularly attend your local mosque
  • Participate in Islamic events
  • Join Muslim student associations
  • Join online Muslim communities
  • Take Islamic courses
  • Volunteer for charitable work

What should I do during Ramadan?

During Ramadan:

  • Wake up for Suhur and eat
  • Abstain from food, drink, and sexual relations during the day
  • Break your fast at Iftar
  • Read more Quran
  • Pray more
  • Give charity
  • Attend Tarawih prayers

Learning and Development Questions

Do I have to learn Arabic?

Learning Arabic is not mandatory, but it is beneficial:

  • Learning basic Arabic supplications for prayer is sufficient
  • Arabic is helpful for understanding the Quran
  • You can read translations in your own language
  • You can try to learn basic Arabic over time

Which books should I read?

Recommended books for new Muslims:

  • Translation of the Holy Quran
  • Biography of Prophet Muhammad (Seerah books)
  • Basic Islamic knowledge books
  • Prayer and worship guides
  • Islamic ethics books

What happens when I make mistakes?

Making mistakes is normal in Islam:

  • Allah is Most Forgiving
  • Sincere repentance is sufficient
  • Learn from your mistakes
  • Don't blame yourself too much
  • Continue learning
  • Strive for perfection but don't expect to be perfect

Practical Advice

For New Muslims

  • Be patient and learn step by step
  • Don't hesitate to ask questions
  • Connect with the local Muslim community
  • Pray regularly

Important Reminders

  • Learning Islam is a lifelong process
  • Every Muslim is at a different level
  • Progress at your own pace
  • Trust in Allah's mercy

Conclusion

These are the questions that new Muslims wonder about most. If you have other questions, don't hesitate to seek help from your local mosque or experienced Muslims.

Remember that learning Islam is a journey. Be patient, ask for Allah's help, and progress step by step.
