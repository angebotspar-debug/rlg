Pillars of Faith

Pillars of Faith

6 minutes read
Beginner Level
For New Muslims

The Pillars of Faith are the fundamental beliefs of Islam. These six pillars consist of faith in Allah, angels, books, prophets, the Day of Judgment, and destiny.

What are the Pillars of Faith?

In Islam, there are six fundamental pillars that we must believe in:

1

Faith in Allah

2

Faith in Angels

3

Faith in Books

4

Faith in Prophets

5

Faith in the Day of Judgment

6

Faith in Destiny

1. Faith in Allah

Faith in Allah is the most fundamental pillar of Islam. This faith includes:

Allah's Existence

  • Allah exists and is One
  • He is the Creator and Owner of everything
  • He has no partner, equal, or associate

Allah's Attributes

  • Ar-Rahman: The Most Merciful
  • Ar-Rahim: The Most Compassionate
  • Al-Aleem: The All-Knowing
  • Al-Qadeer: The All-Powerful

Tawhid (Monotheism)

  • Believing in Allah's unity
  • Worshipping Allah alone
  • Avoiding shirk (associating partners with Allah)

2. Faith in Angels

Angels are pure beings created by Allah from light. They carry out Allah's commands without question.

Gabriel (Jibril)

The angel of revelation. He brings Allah's messages to the prophets.

Michael (Mikail)

The angel of sustenance. He is responsible for rain, wind, and plant growth.

Israfil

The angel of the trumpet. He will blow the trumpet on the Day of Judgment.

Azrael (Izrail)

The angel of death. He is tasked with taking souls.

Kiraman Katibin

The recording angels. They record people's deeds.

Munkar and Nakir

The grave angels. They question the deceased in the grave.

3. Faith in Books

Allah sent books to humanity through prophets to provide guidance.

Torah (Tawrat)

The book revealed to Prophet Moses (Musa)

Psalms (Zabur)

The book revealed to Prophet David (Dawud)

Gospel (Injil)

The book revealed to Prophet Jesus (Isa)

Quran (Qur'an)

The final book revealed to Prophet Muhammad (SAW)

  • Will be preserved until the Day of Judgment
  • Guidance for all humanity
  • Revealed in Arabic

4. Faith in Prophets

Allah sent prophets to humanity to show them the right path. The first prophet was Adam, and the final prophet is Muhammad (SAW).

Ulul Azm Prophets

The five prophets who faced the greatest trials:

  • Noah (Nuh): The prophet of the flood
  • Abraham (Ibrahim): Khalilullah (Friend of Allah)
  • Moses (Musa): Kalimullah (One who spoke with Allah)
  • Jesus (Isa): Ruhullah (Spirit of Allah)
  • Muhammad (SAW): Habibullah (Beloved of Allah)

Prophet Muhammad (SAW)

Special characteristics as the final prophet:

  • Sent to all humanity
  • The Quran was revealed to him
  • His prophethood continues until the Day of Judgment
  • He had the most beautiful character

5. Faith in the Day of Judgment

The Day of Judgment is when this worldly life will end and everyone will be held accountable.

Death

The reality that every living being will taste

Life in the Grave

The period from death until the Day of Judgment

The Day of Judgment

The day when the world will come to an end

Resurrection

The day when all people will be brought back to life

Accounting

The day when deeds will be weighed

Paradise and Hell

The eternal dwelling place

6. Faith in Destiny

Destiny (Qadar) is everything that Allah has known and decreed from eternity.

Knowledge (Ilm)

Allah knows everything from eternity

Writing (Kitabah)

Everything is written in the Preserved Tablet

Will (Mashiah)

Nothing happens without Allah's will

Creation (Khalq)

Allah is the Creator of everything

Important Note

Faith in destiny does not eliminate human responsibility. Humans are responsible for their own choices and actions.

Importance of the Pillars of Faith

Peace of Heart

The pillars of faith give people inner peace and confidence

Right Path

They help in making correct decisions in life

Preparation for the Afterlife

They enable preparation for life after death

Moral Values

They form the foundation of good character and behavior

Conclusion

The Pillars of Faith are the fundamental foundation of Islam. Having sincere faith in these six pillars is a requirement for being a Muslim.

Every Muslim should learn, understand, and live these pillars in their life. The pillars of faith are not only accepted with the mind but must also be confirmed with the heart.
