Prayer Guide

8 min read
Intermediate

Prayer (Salah) is one of the Five Pillars of Islam and is performed five times daily. This guide provides detailed instructions for ablution (Wudu) and performing prayers for new Muslims.

Daily Prayer Times

There are five daily prayers, each with specific times:

1. Fajr (Dawn Prayer)

Time: From dawn until sunrise

Rakats: 2 Sunnah + 2 Fard

2. Dhuhr (Noon Prayer)

Time: From midday until Asr time

Rakats: 4 Sunnah + 4 Fard + 2 Sunnah

3. Asr (Afternoon Prayer)

Time: From afternoon until sunset

Rakats: 4 Sunnah + 4 Fard

4. Maghrib (Sunset Prayer)

Time: From sunset until Isha time

Rakats: 3 Fard + 2 Sunnah

5. Isha (Night Prayer)

Time: From nightfall until dawn

Rakats: 4 Sunnah + 4 Fard + 2 Sunnah + 3 Witr

Ablution (Wudu)

Before praying, one must perform ablution (Wudu). This provides both physical and spiritual purification.

1

Intention

Make the intention in your heart to perform ablution.

2

Bismillah

Say "Bismillahir-Rahmanir-Rahim"

3

Wash Hands

Wash both hands up to the wrists 3 times.

4

Rinse Mouth

Rinse your mouth 3 times.

5

Clean Nose

Sniff water into your nose and blow it out 3 times.

6

Wash Face

Wash your face from ear to ear, forehead to chin, 3 times.

7

Wash Arms

Wash right arm, then left arm up to the elbows, 3 times each.

8

Wipe Head

Wipe over 1/4 of your head with wet hands.

9

Wash Feet

Wash right foot, then left foot up to the ankles, 3 times each.

After performing ablution, pray in a clean place facing the Qibla (direction of Kaaba).

Important Notes

Qibla Direction

  • Qibla is the direction of the Kaaba in Mecca
  • Use a compass or mobile apps to find Qibla
  • From most locations, Qibla is towards the southeast

Cleanliness Requirements

  • Must have performed ablution (Wudu)
  • Wear clean clothes
  • Pray in a clean place
  • Cover the body appropriately (Awrah)

Conclusion

Prayer is the most important connection between a servant and Allah. Although it may seem difficult at first, with regular practice you will become comfortable and find spiritual peace.

Be patient while learning to pray and don't strive for perfection immediately. What matters most is turning to Allah with a sincere heart.
